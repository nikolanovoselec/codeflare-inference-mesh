# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Codeflare Inference Mesh is for operators and infrastructure-minded AI developers who run private inference capacity on machines they already own. They use it during setup, rollout, incident response, model switching, node enrollment, and daily fleet checks.

## Product Purpose

The product turns WARP-connected Linux, macOS, and Windows machines into a private inference mesh behind a Cloudflare Worker control plane. Success means an operator can claim a deployment, protect it with Access, connect AI Gateway, enroll nodes, choose runtimes, observe health, and verify inference without SSH-first debugging.

## Brand Personality

Operator-grade, precise, restrained. The interface should feel like a serious control room for private AI infrastructure: calm under load, direct about state, and confident enough to expose real system details without theatrics.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a generic SaaS onboarding funnel, a friendly marketing wizard, or a decorative dark dashboard. Avoid glossy cards, gradient text as decoration, oversized step ceremony, patronizing explanations, or inconsistent controls that make infrastructure actions feel unserious.

## Design Principles

- Match the operator console: dark zinc surfaces, coral action accents, mono values, tight hierarchy, and explicit system state.
- Get to the first useful operator action quickly; onboarding should guide setup, not teach every feature.
- Prefer real controls, endpoint/status context, and visible consequences over abstract instructions.
- Keep destructive or security-sensitive steps calm, legible, and role-aware.
- Make responsive layouts structural: compact but readable on mobile, not a shrunken desktop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve visible focus states, touch targets of at least 44px, keyboard-friendly form flow, reduced-motion-safe enhancements, and clear status/error feedback for setup actions.
